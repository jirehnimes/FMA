What is Electronic Violence Against Women (eVAW)? It is a gender-based violence committed and perpetrated in the new spaces or arenas. It is embedded in, mediated by or facilitated through ICTs. Perpetrators use ICT to track and harass women, young women and girls, causing emotional distress and fear for their personal safety. The acts includes transmitting threats or false accusations through blogs, in chat rooms, or via mobile phones and other social networking sites. eVAW also includes sending repeated and unwanted communications with sexual undertones, stealing identity or data and spying and monitoring their online activities, computer and internet use without permission. Some these violence escalate into reality and can seriously impair one’s capacity to participate and use ICT without apprehension or fear.
